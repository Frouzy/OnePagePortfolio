- About Me -

Allow me to introduce myself. I am a storyteller, having written and told stories for leading Dutch business media over the past 19 years, of which 13 years at Het FD. In this role I have built an extensive network; won a world-recognized award for journalistic talent and worked as a newsroom manager. I'm at my best when I create (digital) stories, but most of all: I want make a difference. Preferably in a team with people who do not take everything for granted.

In my current role as an innovation journalist, I'm responsible for high-profile stories about big data, artificial intelligence, new business models and more. In addition, I experiment with innovative formats by combining my writing, coding and design skills. And last but not least I'm a presenter of FD events and I organize debates myself.

I'm also the founder of publisher JoMo Media. For my self-written youth novel 'De Geldfabriek' I raised funds through crowdfunding, published the book, and taught children about financial literacy. I also arranged intersting partnerships with De Nederlandsche Bank, Heutink, Citi Group and Fontys Hogeschool Tilburg.

- Skills -

Journalism

I write stories about the latest news and business trends, this is what I have done for the past 19 years. This allowed me to take part in complex debates and make them accessible for a wide audience via my articles.

Strategy

I have built my own company, interviewed c-level managers for many years about their strategic decisions and helped startups to keep their feet on the ground. I am also active as a jury in Start-up Awards, like TEDx Women.

Presenting

For Het Financieele Dagblad I presented different kind of events, like the Gazellen Awards. Also other companies sometimes ask me to lead a debate or present at their event, mostly about a financial or business topic.

Stories

What is a story if nobody reads it? Therefore, I always like to experiment with different genres, like narrative journalism and even a novel. The goal is to write stories with impact. Digital gave me a broad range of new tools to tell the story in an interactive way.

Code

Coding is one of the best things I've learned in recent years. First of all, it's another way to tell stories, but it's also good to learn how to make new products and prototypes in a world that is going digital. I've experienced how enriching it is to understand the nerds.

Teaching

After I wrote the youth novel De Geldfabriek, some weekend and primary schools asked me to teach their children about financial literacy. Earlier, I also taught financial journalism to adult students at the school of journalism in Utrecht.
